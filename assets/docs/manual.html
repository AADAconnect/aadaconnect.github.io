User Manual

Welcome to AADAconnect

Your comprehensive guide to setting up, controlling, and navigating your smart home ecosystem. Get the most out of your connected devices with ease.

1. Navigation

Navigating the app is straightforward using the persistent dock at the bottom of your screen:

  • Home: Your main dashboard containing all devices, quick controls, and status indicators.
  • Search: Quickly locate specific devices by name or ID.
  • Add: Provision and configure new smart devices to your network.
  • Rooms: Organize your devices into distinct physical spaces.
  • More: Access settings, layouts, haptics, and your account.
2. Adding a New Device

Adding a new smart plug to your AADAconnect ecosystem takes only a few moments. Follow these detailed steps to ensure a smooth setup:

  • 1
    Start the Setup: Tap the prominent Add (+) icon in the bottom navigation bar of the dashboard.
  • 2
    Note Your Device ID: The app will generate a unique Device ID (e.g., AADA-1A2B3C). Take a quick note of this ID, as it identifies your specific plug.
  • 3
    Connect to the Plug: Leave the AADAconnect app momentarily and open your phone's Wi-Fi settings. Look for a temporary network named AADA-Plug and connect to it.

    Note: Your phone might warn you that this network has "No Internet Connection." This is completely normal since you are connecting directly to the plug, not a router. Choose to stay connected.
  • 4
    Enter Home Wi-Fi: Return to the AADAconnect app. You will see a configuration form. Enter your home's exact Network Name (SSID) and your Wi-Fi Password. Double-check for typos!
  • 5
    Send Configuration: Tap Configure Device. The app will securely send the credentials to the plug. Wait for the success message before navigating away. Once connected, your new device will automatically appear on your Dashboard!

Troubleshooting: If the app says it cannot reach the device in Step 5, ensure your phone hasn't automatically disconnected from the AADA-Plug Wi-Fi network to switch back to mobile data. Temporarily disabling your cellular data during setup can help prevent this.

3. The Dashboard

Device Cards & Quick Control

Each device is represented by a highly interactive card. A colored dot indicates whether the device is Online, Offline, or Checking.

If Quick Control is enabled in settings, you can tap the circular power icon on the card to instantly turn a device ON or OFF. A pulsing orange glow means the command is pending.

Note: You can long-press a card or tap the three-dot icon to quickly Rename or Remove the device.

Sorting & Layouts

Use the chips at the top of the dashboard to filter devices by Online/Offline. Tap the Sort pill to reorder alphabetically, by status, or by rank.

Go to More > Device Layout to change the grid style between Grid, List, Bento, or Compact to fit your preference.

4. Rooms Management

Grouping devices by Room makes managing large homes incredibly simple. Navigate to the Rooms tab and tap Add Room.

Once inside a room, tap Add Device at the top right to assign existing plugs to this space.

Pro Tip: When viewing a room built from a preset (like Kitchen or Bedroom), AADAconnect automatically applies a beautiful, themed background image to the top of your screen!

5. Control & Features

Manual Power

Tapping anywhere on a Device Card opens the Detailed Control Screen. Tap the large, central power button to toggle the device. It will glow Green when active.

Timers & Schedules Coming Soon

We are working hard to bring advanced timing features in an upcoming update!

Timers: You will be able to set a countdown using an interactive drum picker to automatically turn your device ON, OFF, or Toggle when the timer ends.

Schedules: Automate your daily routines by setting exact times of day and repeating days (e.g., M, T, W) for your devices to trigger automatically.

Smart Automations Coming Soon

Prepare for a truly smart home with upcoming conditional triggers:

  • Sunrise/Sunset: Triggers based on local solar times.
  • Away/Arrive: Uses location tracking to trigger devices when you leave or return home.
  • Power Threshold: Triggers actions if wattage limits are exceeded.
6. Settings & Preferences

AADAconnect allows you to customize how the app feels. Access these options via the More tab in the bottom navigation.

Haptic Feedback: To make digital controls feel physical, AADAconnect uses tactile vibrations. You can adjust the vibration intensity slider in Settings > Haptic Feedback, and choose whether to apply it only to the main power controller or to all buttons across the app.

Account Settings: Tap your Profile Icon on the dashboard to view your server connection status and network latency. From the Settings sheet, you can also securely log out of your account.
